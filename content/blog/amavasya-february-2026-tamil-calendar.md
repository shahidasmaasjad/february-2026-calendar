The term ‘Amavasya’ refers to the new moon day in the Hindu lunar calendar. It is a day when the moon is not visible in the sky. In the Tamil calendar, which is a lunisolar calendar, Amavasya dates are crucial for various religious and cultural practices. This day is believed to be auspicious for performing certain rituals and offerings.

The significance of Amavasya stems from its association with ancestors and the belief that performing specific rituals on this day can bring blessings and peace to departed souls. It’s a time for reflection, remembrance, and honoring those who have passed away. The absence of the moon symbolizes a time of introspection and connecting with the inner self.

Unlike the Gregorian calendar, the Tamil calendar follows the lunar cycle, so the dates of Amavasya vary each month. This is why many people search for the exact Amavasya date for a specific month and year. Understanding the Tamil calendar is essential to accurately pinpoint the Amavasya date.

There are no major variations or types of Amavasya. However, the observance may vary based on regional customs and family traditions. Some families may perform specific pujas or offer food to the ancestors, while others might visit temples or engage in charitable activities.

The date of Amavasya changes each year due to the lunar cycle’s nature. Therefore, the Amavasya date for February 2026 will differ from the dates in other years. It’s important to consult the Tamil calendar for the correct date. This is because the Tamil calendar takes into account the position of the moon and sun.

For the year 2026, the specific date of Amavasya in February can be found by consulting a reliable Tamil calendar. These calendars are readily available online, in print, and through local community resources. Be sure to use a calendar that is accurate and up-to-date.

Key elements include the exact date, the tithi (lunar day), and the nakshatra (constellation) prevailing on that day. These details are important for performing rituals and determining the auspiciousness of the day.

Many people utilize Amavasya for performing Shradh ceremonies, which are rituals performed to honor ancestors. It is considered an excellent day for such practices. Additionally, some individuals observe fasts or engage in spiritual practices to enhance their inner peace.

*Amavasya* is also a time for introspection and self-reflection. Many people use the day to spend time in meditation or prayer. This can help to connect with one’s inner self and find a sense of peace.

When selecting a Tamil calendar, make sure it’s from a reputable source. Ensure that the calendar is specifically a Tamil calendar, as it will provide the most accurate Amavasya date. Cross-referencing with multiple sources is also a good practice.

A common mistake is using a Gregorian calendar to find the Amavasya date, which will not be accurate. Always rely on a Tamil calendar or a resource that specifically references the Tamil calendar.

For beginners, it’s best to consult with elders in the family or a local priest to understand the specific rituals and practices associated with Amavasya. They can provide guidance on the correct way to observe the day.
