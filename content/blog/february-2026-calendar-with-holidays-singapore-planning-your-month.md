* **When is Chinese New Year in 2026?** The exact dates for Chinese New Year 2026 will be announced closer to the time, but it typically falls in late January or early February. If it occurs in February, it will likely include public holidays.
* **Are there any other public holidays in February 2026 besides Chinese New Year?** This depends on the official gazette for 2026. Typically, February has few other public holidays unless Chinese New Year falls within it.
* **How can I find the official list of Singapore holidays for 2026?** The Ministry of Manpower (MOM) website is the definitive source for the official gazetted public holidays in Singapore.
* **Do school holidays in February 2026 align with public holidays?** Often, school breaks are scheduled to coincide with or extend public holidays to create longer vacation periods for students and families. However, this is determined by the Ministry of Education’s academic calendar.
* **What should I do if a public holiday falls on a weekend?** In Singapore, if a public holiday falls on a Saturday, the following Monday is usually a day off in lieu, unless it is also a public holiday. If it falls on a Sunday, the following Monday is generally a day off in lieu. This policy ensures that the holiday benefit is received.
* **Is Valentine’s Day a public holiday in Singapore?** No, Valentine’s Day on February 14th is not a public holiday in Singapore. It is a widely observed cultural and romantic occasion but does not grant a day off work.
