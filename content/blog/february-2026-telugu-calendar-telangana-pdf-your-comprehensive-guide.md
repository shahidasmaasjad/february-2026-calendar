The Telugu calendar is a lunisolar calendar used in Andhra Pradesh and Telangana, India. It follows the traditional Hindu calendar system, which is based on the positions of the sun and moon. Each month begins with the new moon, and the calendar is divided into twelve months, with an intercalary month added in certain years to synchronize the lunar and solar cycles.

Understanding the Telugu calendar is important for observing religious festivals, auspicious timings for events like weddings and housewarmings (muhurthams), and general daily planning according to traditional practices. The core components of a Telugu calendar include the Tithi (lunar day), Nakshatra (lunar mansion), Yoga, and Karana. Each day is characterized by these elements, which are used to determine auspicious timings. For February 2026, understanding these components helps in grasping the nuances of each day.

The **Tithi** represents a specific duration of the lunar day, and there are 30 Tithis in a lunar month. The **Nakshatra**, or lunar mansion, is one of the 27 divisions of the ecliptic, each associated with specific characteristics and timings. The **Yoga** is calculated based on the sun and moon’s positions, and the **Karana** is half of a Tithi.

For instance, a particular wedding might be scheduled based on an auspicious Tithi and Nakshatra, ensuring a smooth and blessed union. Similarly, a housewarming ceremony would be planned for a day with favorable planetary positions and timings indicated by the Telugu calendar.
