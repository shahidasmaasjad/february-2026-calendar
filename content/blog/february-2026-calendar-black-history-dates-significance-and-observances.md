Black History Month, also known as African American History Month, is an annual celebration that takes place throughout February. It serves as a reminder of the struggles and triumphs of Black individuals and communities, highlighting their integral role in shaping American history and culture. The observance grew out of ‘Negro History Week,’ which was established in 1926 by historian Carter G. Woodson and the Association for the Study of Negro Life and History.

The purpose of Black History Month extends beyond mere recognition; it aims to deepen understanding, promote inclusivity, and inspire ongoing dialogue about race and equality. It offers a platform to amplify Black voices, stories, and perspectives, fostering a more comprehensive and equitable historical narrative.

While Black History Month is primarily observed in the United States and Canada, it has gained recognition and is celebrated in various forms around the world. Different countries may focus on specific aspects of Black history relevant to their own contexts.

Black History Month observances can vary significantly depending on the year, location, and community. The focus may also shift depending on current social and political issues.

**Q: Why is Black History Month celebrated in February?**  
A: February was chosen because it coincides with the birthdays of Frederick Douglass and Abraham Lincoln, two figures who significantly impacted the lives of African Americans.

**Q: Who founded Black History Month?**  
A: Carter G. Woodson, a historian and educator, is considered the founder of Black History Month (originally Negro History Week).

**Q: What is the significance of Black History Month?**  
A: It is a time to celebrate the achievements and contributions of African Americans, and to reflect on the history of racial injustice and the ongoing struggle for equality.

**Q: How can I participate in Black History Month?**  
A: You can participate by attending events, reading books, watching documentaries, supporting Black-owned businesses, and engaging in conversations about race and equality.

**Q: Is Black History Month only for Black people?**  
A: No, Black History Month is for everyone. It is an opportunity for people of all backgrounds to learn about and celebrate Black history and culture.

**Q: What are some important topics to explore during Black History Month?**  
A: Topics include the Civil Rights Movement, the Harlem Renaissance, the contributions of Black scientists and artists, and the ongoing fight for social justice.

**Q: Where can I find resources about Black History Month?**  
A: Libraries, museums, educational institutions, and online resources such as the Association for the Study of African American Life and History (ASALH) offer valuable information.
